#### 2.4.1. Electrogoniometry  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[80, 234, 476, 507]]<|/det|>
Bilateral twin axis electrogoniometers (XM65 sensors) were used to measure wrist flexion/ extension and ulnar/radial deviation. The goniometers were taped to the forearm using double- sided medical tape and adhesive tape. The goniometers were positioned while the participants were sitting, elbows at \(90^{\circ}\) with their wrist in neutral and forearms in pronation resting on the desk surface. Reference lines were drawn over anatomical landmarks to enable accurate positioning of the goniometers (Bucholz and Wellman, 1997; Biometrics, 1999). Left and right electrogoniometers were calibrated in a neutral position. Reference position ( \(0^{\circ}\) flexion and deviation) was defined as the wrist angles when the forearms were supported on the tabletop, elbows at \(90^{\circ}\) , forearms in pronation.

#### 2.4.5. Data collection  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[519, 348, 916, 619]]<|/det|>
The \(30\mathrm{s}\) samples were taken at 0, 5, 10 and \(15\mathrm{min}\) during keyboard use and continually during mouse use. Root- mean- square (RMS) values of raw EMG signals were calculated for each of the recorded epochs. A \(20\mathrm{Hz}\) highpass filter eliminated low frequency artifact, such as movement and a \(50\mathrm{Hz}\) notch filter eliminated mains noise. Electromyographic signals were sampled at a rate of \(992\mathrm{Hz}\) . RMS values of raw EMG signals were calculated with an averaging constant of \(65\mathrm{ms}\) , for each recorded sample. Mean RMS values were calculated for each task for each participant. These mean values were represented as a proportion of the reference contraction ( \(\% \mathrm{RC}\) ). Paired \(t\) - tests were applied to mean EMG values ( \(\% \mathrm{RC}\) ) for each task. \(95\%\) confidence intervals of the effect size statistic \((d)\) were calculated.
