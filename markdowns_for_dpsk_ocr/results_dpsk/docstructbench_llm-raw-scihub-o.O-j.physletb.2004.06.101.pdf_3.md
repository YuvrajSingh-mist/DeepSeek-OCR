<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 118, 916, 153]]<|/det|>
For consistency, the time derivative of the constraints of (10) must vanish and hence they must have vanishing Poisson bracket with \(H\) . Using the fundamental Poisson brackets  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[122, 158, 914, 181]]<|/det|>
\[\left[U(x), \Pi^{U}(y)\right] = \delta (x - y), \quad (13)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 186, 670, 204]]<|/det|>
etc., we find that the primary constraints of (10) imply the secondary constraints  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 208, 914, 233]]<|/det|>
\[(\Sigma , \Sigma_{i}) = \left(-\partial_{k} \Pi_{k}^{V}, \epsilon^{ijk} \partial_{j} \left(\Pi_{k}^{B} - m V_{k}\right) - \mu^{2} B_{i}\right). \quad (14)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 238, 916, 288]]<|/det|>
If \(\mu^{2} = 0\) (the Cremmer- Scherk model Lagrangian [1]), the constraints of (14) would become reducible as then \(\partial_{i} \Sigma_{i} = 0\) and only the transverse portions of \(\Sigma_{i}\) are constraints. Furthermore, with \(\mu^{2} \neq 0\) , the requirement \(\dot{\Sigma}_{i} = 0\) leads to a tertiary constraint  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 293, 914, 316]]<|/det|>
\[T_{k} \equiv \mu^{2} \Pi_{k}^{B} = 0 \quad (15)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 321, 485, 338]]<|/det|>
with \(\Sigma_{i}\) and \(T_{k}\) constituting second class constraints as  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 344, 914, 367]]<|/det|>
\[\left[T_{k}(x), \Sigma_{i}(y)\right] = \mu^{4} \delta_{ik} \delta (x - y). \quad (16)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 370, 916, 471]]<|/det|>
All other constraints are first class and no further constraints need to be imposed for consistency. There are consequently five first class constraints \((\Phi^{U}, \Phi_{k}^{A}\) and \(\Sigma\) ) and six second class constraints \((\Sigma_{i}\) and \(T_{k}\) ). The constraints \(\Phi^{U}\) and \(\Sigma\) correspond to the usual gauge transformations \(\delta W_{0} = \partial_{0} \Omega\) , \(\delta W_{i} = \partial_{i} \Omega\) associated with a gauge field \(W_{\mu}\) , while \(\Phi_{k}^{A}\) is associated with the fact that in (12) \(A_{k}\) acts merely as a Lagrange multiplier (i.e., it is not dynamical) and hence its value is completely arbitrary. Suitable gauge conditions associated with the first class constraints are  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 475, 914, 499]]<|/det|>
\[\left(\gamma^{U}, \gamma_{k}^{A}, \gamma^{V}\right) = (U, A_{k}, \partial_{k} V_{k}) = 0. \quad (17)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 503, 740, 521]]<|/det|>
From (10), (14), (15) and (17) it is evident that the only dynamical degrees of freedom are  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 526, 914, 550]]<|/det|>
\[V_{i}^{T} \equiv \left(\delta_{ij} - \partial_{i} \partial_{j} / \partial^{2}\right) V_{j}. \quad (18)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 554, 916, 589]]<|/det|>
We can verify this directly by explicitly eliminating the non- physical degrees of freedom in (4). First, one decomposes \(V_{k}\) , \(A_{k}\) and \(B_{k}\) into transverse \((T)\) and longitudinal \((L)\) parts where  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 593, 914, 614]]<|/det|>
\[\nabla \times \mathbf{V}^{L} \equiv 0 \equiv \nabla \cdot \mathbf{V}^{T}, \quad (19)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 620, 243, 636]]<|/det|>
etc., (4) now becomes  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[121, 640, 914, 690]]<|/det|>
\[\begin{array}{r l} & {2L = \left(\dot{\mathbf{B}}^{L}\right)^{2} - \left(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B}^{L}\right)^{2} + \left[\dot{\mathbf{B}}^{T} - \nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T}\right]^{2} + \left(\dot{\mathbf{V}}^{T}\right)^{2} - \left(\nabla \times \mathbf{V}^{T}\right)^{2} + \left[\dot{\mathbf{V}}^{L} - \nabla U\right]^{2}}\\ & {\qquad +2m\left[\mathbf{V}^{T}\cdot \left(\nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T}\right) + \mathbf{B}^{L}\cdot \dot{\mathbf{V}}^{L} + \mathbf{B}^{T}\cdot \dot{\mathbf{V}}^{T} - \mathbf{B}^{L}\cdot \nabla U\right] + 2\mu^{2}\left[\mathbf{A}^{T}\cdot \mathbf{B}^{T} + \mathbf{A}^{L}\cdot \mathbf{B}^{L}\right].} \end{array} \quad (20)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 695, 549, 713]]<|/det|>
The equations of motion for \(\mathbf{A}^{L}\) and \(U\) , respectively, imply that  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[121, 717, 914, 740]]<|/det|>
\[\mathbf{B}^{L} = 0 = \dot{\mathbf{V}}^{L} - \nabla U, \quad (21)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 746, 202, 762]]<|/det|>
reducing (20) to  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[121, 767, 914, 792]]<|/det|>
\[2L = \left(\dot{\mathbf{V}}^{T}\right)^{2} - \left(\nabla \times \mathbf{V}^{T}\right)^{2} + \left[\dot{\mathbf{B}}^{T} - \nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T}\right]^{2} + 2m\mathbf{V}^{T}\cdot \left(\nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T}\right) + 2m\mathbf{B}^{T}\cdot \dot{\mathbf{V}}^{T} + 2\mu^{2}\mathbf{A}^{T}\cdot \mathbf{B}^{T}. \quad (22)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 797, 124, 812]]<|/det|>
Since  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[121, 818, 914, 842]]<|/det|>
\[\mathbf{A}^{T} \cdot \mathbf{B}^{T} = -(\nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T}) \cdot (\nabla^{2})^{-1}(\nabla \times \mathbf{B}^{T}), \quad (23)\]  

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[78, 848, 417, 865]]<|/det|>
we can eliminate \(\nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T}\) from (22) to obtain  

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[121, 870, 914, 895]]<|/det|>
\[\nabla \times \mathbf{A}^{T} = \dot{\mathbf{B}}^{T} - m \mathbf{V}^{T} + \mu^{2}(\nabla^{2})^{-1}(\nabla \times \mathbf{B}^{T}). \quad (24)\]
