HOMOGENEOUS LINEAR SYSTEMS

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 5 & -4 & 0 \\
-3 & -2 & 4 & 0 \\
6 & 1 & -8 & 0
\end{bmatrix} \sim
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 5 & -4 & 0 \\
0 & 3 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & -9 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix} \sim
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 5 & -4 & 0 \\
0 & 3 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
\]

- Since \( x_3 \) is a free variable, \( Ax = 0 \) has nontrivial solutions (one for each choice of \( x_3 \).)
- Continue the row reduction of \([A \quad 0]\) to *reduced* echelon form:

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
\]

\[
x_1 - \frac{4}{3} x_3 = 0 \\
x_2 = 0 \\
0 = 0
\]
