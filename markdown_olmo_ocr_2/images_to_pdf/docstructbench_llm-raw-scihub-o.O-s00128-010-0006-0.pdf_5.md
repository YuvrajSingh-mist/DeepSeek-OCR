The dominance of ionic species in precipitation was geographically differs from locations and regions are presented in Table 8. In Alibagh [Cl > Na > Mg > Ca > SO<sub>4</sub> > NO<sub>3</sub> > NH<sub>4</sub> > K], Goa [Cl > Na > Ca > Mg > SO<sub>4</sub> > NO<sub>3</sub> > K], and Bombay [Cl > Na > Ca > Mg > SO<sub>4</sub> > NO<sub>3</sub> > K] showed dominance of Cl (Naik et al. 2002; Parashar et al. 2001; Sequeira 1976) whereas at Colaba [Na > Cl > Ca > Mg > SO<sub>4</sub> > NO<sub>3</sub> > NH<sub>4</sub> > K], Silent Valley [Na > Ca > Cl > NO<sub>3</sub> > SO<sub>4</sub> > Mg > K> NH<sub>4</sub>], Chembur [Na > Cl > Ca > NO<sub>3</sub> > NH<sub>4</sub> > Mg > SO<sub>4</sub> > K], there is a dominance of Na as these sites are nearer to sea coast (Naik et al. 2002; Rao et al. 1995; Khemani et al. 1994).
